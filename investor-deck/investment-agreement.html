2. RECITALS
2.1 WHEREAS, the Promoter has acquired approximately 97 Kathas (~1.6 acres) of land with direct Ganga frontage at Uluberia, Howrah District, West Bengal (the "Land"), for the purpose of developing an intergenerational community project known as "RangaMati Vatika" (the "Project").
2.2 WHEREAS, the Project envisages the construction of 75 residential and community units of approximately 1,000 sq.ft. each, comprising 30 school campus units, 30 senior living units, and 15 eco-retreat units, to be offered on 99-year lease terms at a premium of ₹40,00,000 (Rupees Forty Lakhs) per unit.
2.3 WHEREAS, the Promoter has completed the Bhoomi Puja for the Project and now seeks investment of ₹2,50,00,000 (Rupees Two Crores Fifty Lakhs) in two phases for land registration, approvals, infrastructure development, and construction.
2.4 WHEREAS, the Investor, having conducted due diligence on the Project and the Promoter's track record, wishes to invest in the Project on the terms and conditions set forth herein.
3. DEFINITIONS
"Project" means RangaMati Vatika, the intergenerational community development at Uluberia, Howrah, West Bengal.
"Land" means the approximately 97 Kathas (~1.6 acres) of land with Ganga frontage at the Project site, as detailed in Annexure A.
"Unit" means an individual residential or community unit of approximately 1,000 sq.ft. built-up area within the Project.
"Lease Premium" means ₹40,00,000 (Rupees Forty Lakhs) per Unit, payable by end-lessees under 99-year lease terms.
"Phase 1 Investment" means ₹1,00,00,000 (Rupees One Crore) invested for land registration, approvals, and initial infrastructure.
"Phase 2 Investment" means ₹1,50,00,000 (Rupees One Crore Fifty Lakhs) invested for construction and completion.
"Surplus" means the net amount remaining after deducting all Project costs from total revenue, estimated at ₹8,00,00,000 (Rupees Eight Crores).
"Completion" means the date on which all 75 Units have received occupancy certificates or equivalent local approvals.
"SPV" means Special Purpose Vehicle, if formed for the Project under the Companies Act, 2013.

5. LAND ALLOCATION
5.1 In consideration of the Phase 1 Investment, the Promoter shall transfer a 25% (twenty-five percent) undivided share of the Land to the Investor. The land split shall be: 50% Promoter, 25% Investor 1, 25% Investor 2.
5.2 The registration of the Investor's land share shall be completed within 60 (sixty) days of receipt of the Phase 1 Investment, at the applicable Sub-Registrar's office in Howrah District.
5.3 All stamp duty, registration charges, and associated government fees for the Investor's land share shall be borne by the [Promoter/Investor].
5.4 The Promoter represents and warrants that the Land has a clear and marketable title, free from all encumbrances, liens, charges, and disputes, as evidenced by the title documents listed in Annexure A.
6. UNIT ALLOCATION
6.1 The Investor shall be allocated 12 (twelve) Units from the total 75 Units in the Project.
6.2 The Investor shall have the right to select their 12 Units from the master plan, with selection priority based on the order of investment commitment. Selection must be completed within 30 (thirty) days of master plan approval.
6.3 Each allocated Unit shall be offered to end-lessees on 99-year lease terms at a premium of ₹40,00,000 per Unit, with the Investor receiving the full lease premium for their allocated Units.
6.4 The Investor may, at their sole discretion, retain any or all allocated Units for personal use, lease them to third parties, or request the Promoter to market them on their behalf.
7. EQUITY PARTICIPATION
7.1 In addition to the land and unit allocation, the Phase 1 Investor shall hold a 25% (twenty-five percent) equity stake in the Project SPV, if constituted, or an equivalent profit-sharing arrangement.
7.2 The equity participation entitles the Investor to 25% of all Project surplus beyond the direct unit revenues, including but not limited to: community fees, common area revenues, and any appreciation in land value.
7.3 The Promoter shall maintain a separate Project account and provide quarterly financial statements to all equity holders.
8. RETURNS & EXIT
8.1 Phase 1 Investment Returns: The Promoter projects a return of 2.0x (two times) the Phase 1 Investment of ₹1,00,00,000, generating ₹2,00,00,000 through a combination of unit lease premiums and equity participation.
8.2 Phase 2 Investment Returns: The projected return on Phase 2 Investment of ₹1,50,00,000 is 1.33x (one point three three times), generating ₹2,00,00,000.
8.3 Returns shall be distributed as Units are leased and payments are received from end-lessees. The Promoter shall distribute the Investor's share within 15 (fifteen) days of each lease premium receipt.
8.4 Exit Mechanism: The Investor may exit the Project at any time after the 12-month lock-in period by:
Selling their land share and/or unit allocation to a third party (with Promoter's right of first refusal);
Selling their equity stake to the Promoter or another investor at fair market value;
Mutual buy-out at a price determined by an independent valuer agreed by both Parties.
9. PROMOTER OBLIGATIONS
9.1 The Promoter shall complete the Project within 18 (eighteen) months from the date of receipt of the Phase 1 Investment, subject to force majeure events.
9.2 The Promoter shall obtain all necessary governmental approvals, permits, and licenses required for the development and operation of the Project.
9.3 The Promoter shall ensure construction quality meets or exceeds applicable BIS standards and local building code requirements.
9.4 The Promoter shall provide the Investor with:
Monthly construction progress reports with photographs;
Quarterly financial statements of the Project account;
Immediate notification of any material adverse change or regulatory issue;
Access to the Project site for inspection upon 24 hours' prior notice.
10. INVESTOR RIGHTS
10.1 Information Rights: The Investor shall have the right to inspect all Project-related books, records, and accounts, upon reasonable notice.
10.2 Site Access: The Investor shall have the right to visit the Project site at any time during normal business hours, with prior intimation.
10.3 Audit Rights: The Investor may appoint an independent auditor to audit Project finances once per financial year, at the Investor's cost.
10.4 Board/Advisory Seat: The Phase 1 Investor shall be entitled to a seat on the Project advisory board (or Board of Directors if an SPV is formed).
10.5 Consent Rights: The following actions shall require the Investor's prior written consent:
Any change in the Project scope, design, or unit pricing;
Borrowing exceeding ₹25,00,000 against the Project;
Sale or encumbrance of Project land beyond allocated shares;
Entry of additional investors or change in the equity structure.

The Investor has conducted independent due diligence and is making an informed investment decision;
The Investor understands that projected returns are estimates and not guarantees.
12. DISPUTE RESOLUTION
12.1 The Parties shall first attempt to resolve any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement through good-faith negotiation within 30 (thirty) days of written notice of the dispute.
12.2 If the dispute is not resolved through negotiation, it shall be referred to and finally resolved by arbitration under the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, as amended.
12.3 The arbitration shall be conducted by a sole arbitrator mutually agreed upon by the Parties. If the Parties cannot agree on an arbitrator within 15 (fifteen) days, the arbitrator shall be appointed by the High Court of Calcutta.
12.4 The seat and venue of arbitration shall be Kolkata, West Bengal, India. The language of arbitration shall be English.
12.5 The courts of Kolkata shall have exclusive jurisdiction over any matters not subject to arbitration.
13. CONFIDENTIALITY
13.1 Each Party agrees to maintain strict confidentiality regarding the terms of this Agreement, all financial information, business plans, and proprietary information shared in connection with the Project.
13.2 This confidentiality obligation shall survive the termination of this Agreement for a period of 3 (three) years.
13.3 Notwithstanding the above, disclosure shall be permitted where required by law, regulation, court order, or governmental authority, provided the disclosing Party gives prior written notice to the other Party where legally permissible.
14. GENERAL PROVISIONS
14.1 Governing Law: This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of India, specifically the Indian Contract Act, 1872, and other applicable legislation.
14.2 Notices: All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and sent by registered post, courier, or email to the addresses specified herein, with copies to the respective legal counsel.
14.3 Amendments: No amendment or modification of this Agreement shall be valid unless made in writing and signed by both Parties.
14.4 Entire Agreement: This Agreement, together with its Annexures, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, and agreements.
14.5 Severability: If any provision of this Agreement is found to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.
14.6 Force Majeure: Neither Party shall be liable for delay or failure in performance caused by events beyond reasonable control, including but not limited to natural disasters, wars, epidemics, government actions, or regulatory changes.
14.7 Waiver: No failure or delay by either Party in exercising any right under this Agreement shall constitute a waiver of that right.
